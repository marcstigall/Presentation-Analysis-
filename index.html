The First 21 Days of a Bee's Life

Link to Video

What made this a great presentation was the visuals and the audio. Music was used to create an atmosphere depending on the part of the presentation the speaker was doing. I’m an avid nature enthusiast, so watching this was not only fun and interesting but informative and enlightening on a subject that seems simple but is complex. The speaker told a story about his relationship with bees. After studying them he concluded that the species faces a lot of problems, but one stood out. The simplicity of the subject was appealing and straight to the point. Bees face a mite problem, that when they are attached to a bee it sucks their blood causing immune deficiency which can destroy a colony. This hit home because I love nature and bees are important to our food industry. Bringing awareness about something I never heard of drew me in to learn a new facet in bee life. Watching this presentation instead of just reading showed me firsthand what the mites looked like. 21 days of the bee’s life was condensed to 60 seconds and I was able to see the mites crawling on them as they formed. It was very interesting to watch the beginning of their life. He showed a major concern for the species by not only bringing forth the awareness of the mite problem but also what was being done about it. Treating the mites with chemicals deemed not ideal. So, after observations were made it was concluded that some bees showed a resistance to the mites. The idea of breeding those bees came about. Even still more problems arose with this tactic.

Where is Cybercrime Really Coming From?

The opening is powerful. “Cybercrime is out of control” made me ask questions like; what is cybercrime? How exactly is it out of control? Technology has always interested me, especially software development. Hearing someone talk about how bad guys are using software to infiltrate systems and steal sensitive data is very interesting. The speaker did a great job of explaining the situation and shedding light on where these attacks are coming from. The facts itself drew me in. I had multiple “I didn’t know that” moments. Me wanting to pursue a career in the technology industry showed me that cybercrime is not only real but in desperate need for security specialists to fend off these attacks. The speaker made aware that 80% of cybercrimes come from criminal gangs. These groups collectively have topped out at 445 billion dollars in this illegal economy. Me watching this I’m able to feel the seriousness of the situation. It’s quite scary to find out that the internet can be used in a way to ruin people’s lives financially. The speaker was giving the audience a wake-up call. Letting them know not only what criminals have done so far but also their capabilities. It was kind of humorous at times. Not in a comedic way but in a I can’t believe it kind of way. Showing some emails and tactics that criminals use on victims to get their information baffles people at how real and sophisticated these attacks are.

Qualities and Techniques

The First 21 Days of a Bee's Life

  • The technique of using music to create an atmosphere at different points in the video was a great way to make me feel what he intended. During the 60 seconds timelapse it made the view more enjoyable.
  • The presenter started the presentation with a story that drew me in. He began talking of the bee farm he has in his back yard.
  • Ending the presentation with a call to action on what happens next was inspiring. Stating that to improve the life of the bees we must improve our relationship with them.
  • Having the right amount of hand gestures and eye contact kept me interested and not distracted from the message. I could feel his concern and his seriousness for what he was talking about.

Where is Cybercrime Really Coming From?

  • One of the qualities the speaker shown to me was a sense of humor at a dark situation. Making me laugh was a great way to keep me interested. I thought it was funny that his team encountered a criminal group after reviewing their attack software. They received an email from the criminals boasting about their new and improved product.
  • Hearing that the economy of cybercrime has brought in 445 billion dollars blew me away. This fact alone is enough to make me want to learn more about the subject. The technique of providing quality facts is a wonderful way to make a powerful presentation.
  • The speaker’s call to action is a great way to combat cybercrime. He suggested sharing information on attacks instead of harboring it. With organizations sharing how the attacks happened it can help prevent future losses. Thus, making cybercrime not pay.

Origami Robots That Reshape and Transform Themselves

  • Showing videos of the robots was a very great technique to use on this subject. Showing the possibilities and functions of the robots made me feel invested in the idea.
